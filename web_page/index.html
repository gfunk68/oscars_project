About the Project:


This year, the 92nd Academy Awards saw the first non-English-language film win Best Picture. In recent years, many have criticized the Academy Awards for its lack of diversity in crowning women and people of color for some of its most coveted prizes, particularly Best Director and Best Actors and Actresses awards.

A viral call-to-action hashtag, #OscarsSoWhite turned into a social movement to spread awareness about lack of diversity at the Oscars. Thus, we were inspired to create some visualizations to learn more about the demographics of previous Oscar winners.

Winners' Birthplaces

The vast majority of Oscars winners in these categories over the last 92 years have been from (as expected) New York City and Los Angeles. Surprisingly, none have come from South America or Africa. Quite a few have come from Europe, with the vast majority born in England.

Age & Gender Observations:

  • The majority of Oscar actress winners are younger. Most won their awards when they were under 35 years old.

  • Most Oscar actor and director winners win their awards between age 35 to age 60.

  • The oldest Oscar winner was an actor. He was 82 years old at the time of winning.

  • The youngest Oscar winner was an actress. She was 10 years old at the time of winning.

  • The average Oscar-winning age for all groups has increased over time.

  • Kathryn Bigelow is the only woman to have ever won Best Director for 2009's The Hurt Locker.
